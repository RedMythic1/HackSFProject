What is the concept of amicus curiae brief?

An amicus curiae brief, also known as a friend of the court brief, is a legal document that is filed in a case by a non-party who has an interest in the outcome of the case. The purpose of the brief is to provide the court with additional information or arguments that may not be presented by the parties to the case. The amicus curiae brief is meant to assist the court in making a well-informed decision, and it is often used in cases where the issue presented has important implications for society or the law. Amicus curiae briefs are typically filed by organizations or individuals who have a vested interest in the outcome of the case but are not directly affected by it.
