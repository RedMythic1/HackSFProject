PLAttice is designed to make square box trusses that can double up side-to-side or branch off at 60 degree angles as needed. It is anisotropic and limited as compared to systems based on cubic stacking arrangements, but works well for forms that are long and spindly and perhaps a bit tree-like. The nodes each host up to ten struts, but in practice a typical box truss only uses six joints per node. PLAttice comes with a few extra bits that help structures fit into the built environment, including end nodes and short struts that square off the end of a box truss, and can be bolted onto a routed plywood panel to make a nice foot, backed up with self-adhesive rubber pads for extra friction against the floor. The feet also include a self-adhesive rubber pad for ceiling friction. Installing the feet isn't terrific, since the pad rotates during adjustment, causing the tower to walk around a bit. However, the structure is simple and easy to build, making it a great option for those who want to construct an overhead lamp for a kitchen table but don't want to put a fussy hook into the ceiling.
